Connecting Global Youth

Welcome to my camp story! Growing up, I discovered that the most valuable connections weren't built across dynamic digital messaging platforms, but around the soft warmth of a shared crackling campfire. This exact realization inspired me to build the foundations of Frog Toad Lake.

My operations strategy focuses heavily on creating safe, cozy physical ecosystems where young travelers from over 45 represented nations can learn about their neighbors while navigating wild, untamed territories. I balance administrative camp mapping with active field coaching.

"Nature is the ultimate, non-judgmental classroom. When you strip away notifications, real communication and character emerge organically."

Preparedness is Inner Peace

Hello, adventurers! I have spent over a decade leading deep-country rescue patrols and environmental education groups. For me, teaching survival skill sets isn't about fostering panic—it is about cultivating a quiet, unwavering confidence in the face of any weather pattern or navigation trial.

At Frog Toad Lake, I work directly with campers to identify useful forest flora, map terrain using stars and simple analog instruments, and design completely windproof shelters. Watching campers shift from feeling intimidated by nature to feeling cozy and secure inside it is my greatest reward.

"True bushcraft is not about fighting the forest; it is about learning its language so you can rest comfortably in its heart."

The Joy of Team Play

Welcome! I'm Maya, and I coordinate everything that moves, swims, runs, and cheers here at the lake. As a former competitive athletic director, I design water safety programs and team field games that build absolute fitness alongside deep collaborative trust.

At Frog Toad Lake, we transform traditional athletics into immersive team experiences. Whether it's organizing full lake regattas or staging massive forest hide-and-seek tournaments, my design focuses on ensuring that every camper—regardless of background—feels the thrill of shared victory and fun.

"You can learn more about a teammate during an hour of outdoor games than in a lifetime of standard conversation. Play breaks all barriers."
